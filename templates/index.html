About

This is a project that reports the minimum wage in the United States by state and year, and compares it with the cost of living in each state.

All minimum wage data has been adjusted for inflation, meaning to equal the value of a dollar in 2022. The Cost of Living Index (COLI) is a measurement of the basic expenses of a location. It is also known as the Consumer Price Index. The cost of living of an average city in the country is set to 100.0 and every other location is assessed based on that scale. For example, in 2023, New York State has a COLI of 148.2, meaning it is 48.2% more expensive to live in New York than in an average state.


Data Pipeline:

  1. Copied Kaggle data (min wage 1969-2020) onto a Google sheet and removed all columns excluding state, year, state minimum wage (2020 adjusted), and federal minimum wage (2020 adjusted)
  2. Formatted Labor Law Center data (min wage 2021-2022, unadjusted) to match Kaggle data and combined the two spreadsheets.
  3. Adjusted all 2020 and 2021 values to 2022 inflation by applying a 13.1% and 8.0% increase, respectively.
  4. Copied 2022 MERIC data (COLI by state and expense) onto a separate spreadsheet.
  5. In formatData.py, created a dictionary to store data and used readlines() to generate a list of the lines of MERIC data.
  6. For each line, created a dictionary key that is a dictionary of the state. As values, added dictionaries for both "CPI" and "years" data.
  7. Added data to "CPI" dictionary with the keys as the expense and the values as the cost index.
  8. Used readlines() on the other spreadsheet.
  9. Added to the "years" dictionary with the year as the key and a list containing the corresponding state and federal minimum wage as the value.
